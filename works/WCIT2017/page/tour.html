Multiculturalism of Taipei

Day 1 Designated Taipei City Location>Longshan Temple>Ximending Fashion District>Chiang-Kai-Shek Memorial Hall>Back to Taipei
0900-0930 Designated place set
1000-1330 Longshan Temple
Mengjia Longshan Temple is considered by many to be an archetypal example of classical Taiwanese architecture. Built in 1738 by early settlers from Fujian, China, the temple serves as a place of worship and gathering for local residents. Like most temples around Taiwan, there is a mixture of worship to Buddhist and Taoist deities.

Ximending Fashion District
Ximending is a neighborhood and shopping district in the Wanhua District of Taipei. It was the first pedestrian zone in Taiwan, and was the original source of Taiwan's fashion and subculture before the newer commercial zones in the Eastern District were established. Ximending remains the most important consumer district in the Western District of Taipei.
1330-1500 Chiang-Kai-Shek Memorial Hall
Located in Zhongzheng District of Taipei, the Chiang-Kai-Shek Memorial Hall was erected in 1980 to commemorate the former President of the Republic of China. The ground level houses a library and museum documenting Chiang-Kai-shek's life, while the upper level is where his statue can be found. A guard mounting ceremony is scheduled on the hour everyday between 9:00 ~ 17:00.
1500- Go home

0900-1130 Beitou Thermal Valley
Beitou thermal valley is a volcanic crater filled with steaming natural sulfuric hot spring water. During the Japanese colonial era, the valley was known as one of Taiwan’s eight great natural beauties.

Beitou hot spring museum was originally constructed by the Japanese in 1913. It has been listed the third grade ancient monument of Taiwan.
